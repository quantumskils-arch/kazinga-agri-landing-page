Beekeeping 101: Start Your First Hive

✍️ Kazinga Team📅 June 2026⏱ 6 min read

Beekeeping is one of the easiest home businesses to start in Uganda. Bees do most of the work — you simply give them a good home, protect them, and harvest the honey. With just one or two hives in your backyard, you can earn a steady income from honey, beeswax and propolis.

Why Beekeeping?

  • Low maintenance — bees feed themselves from flowers around you.
  • Small space — a corner of your compound is enough to begin.
  • High demand — honey sells fast and at good prices.
  • Multiple products — honey, wax, propolis and even pollination services.

What You Need to Start

Getting started is simpler than most people think. Here's your basic shopping list:

  • 1–2 hives (Langstroth or Kenyan Top Bar work well locally)
  • A bee suit, gloves and a veil for safety
  • A smoker to calm the bees during inspection
  • A hive tool and a clean bucket for harvesting
  • A shady, quiet spot away from heavy foot traffic

Budget tip: A starter setup costs around UGX 250,000. Many of our members fund this with a small VSLA loan and repay it from their first harvests.

Setting Up Your Hive

Place the hive about knee-height on a stand to protect it from ants and dampness. Face the entrance away from strong winds. Bees will often find your hive naturally, but you can also attract a colony by baiting the hive with beeswax.

Caring for Your Bees

Inspect calmly every two to three weeks, ideally in the cool of early morning or late evening. Always wear protective gear and use your smoker gently. Keep the area clean and make sure there's water and flowering plants nearby.

Harvesting Your First Honey

A new colony usually produces harvestable honey after about four to six months. Harvest only the capped, ripe combs and always leave enough honey for the bees to survive. Strain the honey cleanly, bottle it, and you're ready to sell.
